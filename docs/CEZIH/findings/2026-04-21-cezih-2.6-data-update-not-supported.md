## Hypotheses (ranked by likelihood)

Successful 2.4 Resolve and 2.9 Reopen on this SAME case use a stripped-down
`build_condition_status_update` that emits ONLY:
- `identifier` (ID_CASE_GLOBAL, same value)
- `subject` (MBO)
- `clinicalStatus` (`resolved` for 2.4; omitted for 2.9)
- `abatementDateTime` (2.4 only)

Our 2.6 `build_condition_data_update` additionally emits: `code`, `onsetDateTime`,
`asserter`, `verificationStatus`, `note`. The delta between working and failing
payloads is in these five fields. In order of likelihood:

1. **`asserter` forbidden for 2.6** â€” status-update flows don't emit it. CEZIH
   may slice the 2.6 Condition profile to forbid asserter on updates (the
   original asserter was pinned at create; updating it via 2.6 may be illegal).
2. **`code` (ICD) forbidden for 2.6 when unchanged** â€” CEZIH may reject a
   2.6 message that re-sends the existing ICD code rather than omitting it
   when unchanged. Only fields that actually change may be allowed.
3. **`onsetDateTime` slice mismatch** â€” our onset format is ISO with timezone;
   CEZIH may want date-only.
4. **`clinicalStatus` required but different CodeSystem** â€” we send
   `terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/condition-clinical`; the CEZIH profile may
   require `fhir.cezih.hr/.../clinical-status` instead.
5. **`note.extension` invariant** â€” our note always sends annotation-type="4"
   (biljeĹˇka). Spec may require different code for updates.
